76-6-201.   Definitions.
As used in this part:
(1) (a)  "Building," in addition to its ordinary meaning, means any
watercraft, aircraft, trailer, or other structure or vehicle adapted for
overnight accommodation of persons or for carrying on business and includes:
(i)  each separately secured or occupied portion of the structure or vehicle;
and
(ii)  each structure appurtenant to or connected with the structure or
vehicle.
(b)  "Building" does not include a railroad car.
(2)  "Dwelling" means a building which is usually occupied by a person lodging
in the building at night, whether or not a person is actually present.
(3)  "Enter or remain unlawfully" means a person enters or remains in or on
any premises when:
(a)  at the time of the entry or remaining, the premises or any portion of the
premises are not open to the public; and
(b)  the actor is not otherwise licensed or privileged to enter or remain on
the premises or any portion of the premises.
(4)  "Enter" means:
(a)  intrusion of any part of the body; or
(b)  intrusion of any physical object under control of the actor.
(5)  "Railroad car":
(a)  in addition to its ordinary meaning, includes a sleeping car or any
container or trailer that is on a railroad car; and
(b)  includes only a railroad car that is operable and part of an ongoing
railroad operation.
