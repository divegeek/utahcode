76-8-503.   False or inconsistent statements.
A person is guilty of a class B misdemeanor if:
(1) (a)  he makes a false statement under oath or affirmation or swears or
affirms the truth of the statement previously made and he does not believe the
statement to be true if:
(i)  the falsification occurs in an official proceeding, or is made with a
purpose to mislead a public servant in performing his official functions; or
(ii)  the statement is one which is authorized by law to be sworn or affirmed
before a notary or other person authorized to administer oaths; or
(b)  he makes inconsistent statements under oath or affirmation, both within
the period of limitations, one of which is false and not believed by him to be
true.
(2)  A person is not guilty under this section if the falsification is
retracted before it becomes manifest that the falsification was or would be
exposed.
