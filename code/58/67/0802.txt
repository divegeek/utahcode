58-67-802.   Form of practice.
(1)  A physician and surgeon licensed under this chapter may engage in
practice as a physician and surgeon, or in the practice of medicine only as an
individual licensee; but as an individual licensee, he may be:
(a)  an individual operating as a business proprietor;
(b)  an employee of another person;
(c)  a partner in a lawfully organized partnership;
(d)  a lawfully formed professional corporation;
(e)  a lawfully organized limited liability company;
(f)  a lawfully organized business corporation; or
(g)  any other form of organization recognized by the state which is not
prohibited by division rule made in collaboration with the board.
(2)  Regardless of the form in which a licensee engages in the practice of
medicine, the licensee may only permit the practice of medicine in that form
of practice to be conducted by an individual:
(a)  licensed in Utah as a physician and surgeon under Section 58-67-301 or as
an osteopathic physician and surgeon under Section 58-68-301; and
(b)  who is able to lawfully and competently engage in the practice of
medicine.
