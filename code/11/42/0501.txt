11-42-501.   Assessment constitutes a lien -- Characteristics of an
assessment lien.
(1)  Each assessment levied under this chapter, including any installment of
an assessment, interest, and any penalties and costs of collection,
constitutes a lien against the property assessed as of the effective date of
the assessment resolution or ordinance.
(2)  A lien under this section:
(a)  is superior to the lien of a trust deed, mortgage, mechanic's or
materialman's lien, or other encumbrances;
(b)  is equal to and on a parity with a lien for general property taxes;
(c)  applies without interruption, change in priority, or alteration in any
manner to any reduced payment obligations; and
(d)  continues until the assessments, reduced payment obligations, and any
interest, penalties, and costs are paid, despite a sale of the property for or
on account of a delinquent general property tax, special tax, or other
assessment or the issuance of a tax deed, an assignment of interest by the
county, or a sheriff's certificate of sale or deed.
