67-4a-210.   Property held by courts and public agencies.
(1)  Any intangible property held by the executive, legislative, or judicial
branch of the United States government, or a state or a county or municipal
subdivision of a state, or any of their authorities, agencies,
instrumentalities, administrations, services, or other organizations that
remains unclaimed for more than one year after it became payable or
distributable is considered abandoned.
(2)  Property held, issued, or owing by the court is payable or distributable
if:
(a)  the court has notified all persons whose names appear on the records of
the court as having an unadjudicated claim to the property that the property
is being held subject to the claim; and
(b)  no claim is made, or property remains after all claims are resolved.
(3)  A claim filed under Section 67-4a-501 for property reported by the court
to the administrator under this Section may be referred to the court for
adjudication of the claim.
