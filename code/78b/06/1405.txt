78B-6-1405.   Counter actions -- Attorney fees -- Damages.
(1)  A defendant in an action involving public participation in the process of
government may maintain an action, claim, cross-claim, or counterclaim to
recover:
(a)  costs and reasonable attorney fees, upon a demonstration that the action
involving public participation in the process of government was commenced or
continued without a substantial basis in fact and law and could not be
supported by a substantial argument for the extension, modification, or
reversal of existing law; and
(b)  other compensatory damages upon an additional demonstration that the
action involving public participation in the process of government was
commenced or continued for the purpose of harassing, intimidating, punishing,
or otherwise maliciously inhibiting the free exercise of rights granted under
the First Amendment to the U.S. Constitution.
(2)  Nothing in this section shall affect or preclude the right of any party
to any recovery otherwise authorized by law.
