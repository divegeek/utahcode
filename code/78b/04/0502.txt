78B-4-502.   Donation of food -- Liability limits.
(1)  A person or entity who donates apparently wholesome food to a nonprofit
organization for distribution to the needy is not subject to civil or criminal
liability regarding the condition of the food unless an injury or death
results from an act or omission of the donor that constitutes gross
negligence, recklessness, or intentional misconduct.
(2)  A nonprofit organization that distributes either directly or indirectly
apparently wholesome food to persons in need at no charge and substantially
complies with applicable local, county, state, and federal laws and
regulations regarding the storage and handling of food for public distribution
is not subject to civil or criminal liability regarding the condition of the
food unless an injury or death results from an act or omission of the
organization that constitutes gross negligence, recklessness, or intentional
misconduct.
