78B-14-312.   Nondisclosure of information in exceptional circumstances.
If a party alleges in an affidavit or a pleading under oath that the health,
safety, or liberty of a party or child would be jeopardized by disclosure of
specific identifying information, that information must be sealed and may not
be disclosed to the other party or the public.  After a hearing in which a
tribunal takes into consideration the health, safety, or liberty of the party
or child, the tribunal may order disclosure of information that the tribunal
determines to be in the interest of justice.
