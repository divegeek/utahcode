69-1-4.   Transmitting certified instruments -- Burden of proof.
Except as hereinbefore otherwise provided, any instrument in writing, duly
certified under his hand and official seal by a notary public, commissioner of
deeds or clerk of a court of record to be genuine to the personal knowledge of
such officer, may, together with such certificate, be sent by telegraph or
telephone. The telegraphic or telephonic copy thereof shall, prima facie only,
have the same force, effect and validity in all respects as the original, and
the burden of proof shall be on the party denying the genuineness or due
execution of the original.
