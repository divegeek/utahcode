13-37-202.   Disclosure of nonpublic personal information prohibited without
notice.
(1)  A commercial entity may not disclose nonpublic personal information that
the commercial entity obtained on or after January 1, 2004, as a result of a
consumer transaction if the commercial entity fails to comply with Section
13-37-201.
(2)  This chapter may not be interpreted as authorizing a commercial entity to
disclose nonpublic personal information to a greater extent than the
commercial entity is otherwise permitted to disclose nonpublic personal
information.
