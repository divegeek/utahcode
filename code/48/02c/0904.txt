48-2c-904.   Valuation of member's interest in the company.
Except as otherwise provided in the operating agreement, the fair market value
of a member's interest in the company at any given time shall be the value at
which the interest would change hands in an arms-length transaction between an
informed and willing buyer and an informed and willing seller, neither being
under any compulsion to buy or to sell, taking into consideration all relevant
facts and circumstances, including the provisions of the articles of
organization and operating agreement and all relevant discounts or premiums.
