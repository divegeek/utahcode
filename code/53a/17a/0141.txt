53A-17a-141.   Alternative programs.
(1)  Since the State Board of Education has adopted a policy that requires
districts to grant credit for proficiency through alternative programs, school
districts are encouraged to continue and expand their cooperation with
accredited institutions through performance contracts for educational
services, particularly where it is beneficial to students whose progress could
be better served through alternative programs.
(2)  School districts are encouraged to participate in programs that focus on
increasing the number of ethnic minority and female students in the secondary
schools who will go on to study mathematics, engineering, or related sciences
at an institution of higher education.
