72-3-103.   County roads -- Class B roads -- Construction and maintenance by
counties.
(1)  County roads comprise all public highways, roads, and streets within the
state that:
(a) are situated outside of incorporated municipalities and not designated as
state highways;
(b) have been designated as county roads; or
(c) are located on property under the control of a federal agency and
constructed or maintained by the county under agreement with the appropriate
federal agency.
(2)  County roads are class B roads.
(3)  The state and county have joint undivided interest in the title to all
rights-of-way for all county roads.
(4) The county governing body exercises sole jurisdiction and control of
county roads within the county.
(5)  The county shall construct and maintain each county road using funds made
available for that purpose.
(6)  The county legislative body may expend funds allocated to each county
from the Transportation Fund under rules made by the department.
(7)  A county legislative body may use any portion of the class B road funds
provided by this chapter for the construction and maintenance of class A state
roads by cooperative agreement with the department.
(8)  A county may enter into agreements with the appropriate federal agency
for the use of federal funds, county road funds, and donations to county road
funds to construct, improve, or maintain county roads within or partly within
national forests.
