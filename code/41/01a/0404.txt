41-1a-404.   Location and position of plates.
(1)  License plates issued for a vehicle other than a motorcycle, trailer, or
semitrailer shall be attached to the vehicle, one in the front and the other
in the rear.
(2)  The license plate issued for a motorcycle, trailer, or semitrailer shall
be attached to the rear of the motorcycle, trailer, or semitrailer.
(3)  Every license plate shall at all times be:
(a)  securely fastened:
(i)  in a horizontal position to the vehicle for which it is issued to prevent
the plate from swinging;
(ii)  at a height of not less than 12 inches from the ground, measuring from
the bottom of the plate; and
(iii)  in a place and position to be clearly visible; and
(b)  maintained:
(i)  free from foreign materials; and
(ii)  in a condition to be clearly legible.
(4)  Enforcement by a state or local law enforcement officer of the
requirement under Subsection (1) to attach a license plate to the front of a
vehicle shall be only as a secondary action when the vehicle has been detained
for a suspected violation by any person in the vehicle of Title 41, Motor
Vehicles, other than the requirement under Subsection (1) to attach a license
plate to the front of the vehicle, or for another offense.
