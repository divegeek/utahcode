70A-8-208.    Effect of signature of authenticating trustee, registrar, or
transfer agent.
(1)  A person signing a security certificate as authenticating trustee,
registrar, transfer agent, or the like, warrants to a purchaser for value of
the certificated security, if the purchaser is without notice of a particular
defect, that:
(a)  the certificate is genuine;
(b)  the person's own participation in the issue of the security is within the
person's capacity and within the scope of the authority received by the person
from the issuer; and
(c)  the person has reasonable grounds to believe that the certificated
security is in the form and within the amount the issuer is authorized to
issue.
(2)  Unless otherwise agreed, a person signing under Subsection (1) does not
assume responsibility for the validity of the security in other respects.
