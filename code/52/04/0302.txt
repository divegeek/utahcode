52-4-302.   Suit to void final action -- Limitation -- Exceptions.
(1) (a)  Any final action taken in violation of Section 52-4-201, 52-4-202, or
52-4-207 is voidable by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(b)  A court may not void a final action taken by a public body for failure to
comply with the posting written notice requirements under Subsection
52-4-202(3)(a)(i)(B) if:
(i)  the posting is made for a meeting that is held before April 1, 2009; or
(ii) (A)  the public body otherwise complies with the provisions of Section
52-4-202; and
(B)  the failure was a result of unforeseen Internet hosting or communication
technology failure.
(2)  Except as provided under Subsection (3), a suit to void final action
shall be commenced within 90 days after the date of the action.
(3)  A suit to void final action concerning the issuance of bonds, notes, or
other evidences of indebtedness shall be commenced within 30 days after the
date of the action.
