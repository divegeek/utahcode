30-1-4.5.   Validity of marriage not solemnized.
(1)  A marriage which is not solemnized according to this chapter shall be
legal and valid if a court or administrative order establishes that it arises
out of a contract between a man and a woman who:
(a)  are of legal age and capable of giving consent;
(b)  are legally capable of entering a solemnized marriage under the
provisions of this chapter;
(c)  have cohabited;
(d)  mutually assume marital rights, duties, and obligations; and
(e)  who hold themselves out as and have acquired a uniform and general
reputation as husband and wife.
(2)  The determination or establishment of a marriage under this section must
occur during the relationship described in Subsection (1), or within one year
following the termination of that relationship.  Evidence of a marriage
recognizable under this section may be manifested in any form, and may be
proved under the same general rules of evidence as facts in other cases.
