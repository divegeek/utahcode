62A-15-614.   Clinical director -- Appointment -- Conditions and procedure
-- Duties.
(1)  Whenever the  superintendent is not qualified to be the clinical director
of the state hospital under this section, he shall, with the approval of the
director of the division, appoint a clinical director who is licensed to
practice medicine and surgery in this state, and who has had at least three
years' training in a psychiatric residency program approved by the American
Board of Psychiatry and Neurology, Inc., and who is eligible for certification
by that board.
(2)  The salary of the clinical director of the state hospital shall be fixed
by the standards of the Division of Finance, to be paid in the same manner as
the salaries of other employees.  The clinical director shall perform such
duties as directed by the  superintendent and prescribed by the rules of the
board, and shall prescribe and direct the treatment of patients and adopt
sanitary measures for their welfare.
(3)  If the  superintendent is qualified to be the clinical director, he may
assume the duties of the clinical director.
