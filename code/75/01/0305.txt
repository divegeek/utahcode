75-1-305.   Records and certified copies.
The clerk of the court shall keep a record for each decedent, ward, protected
person, or trust involved in any document which may be filed with the court
under this code, including petitions and applications, demands for notices or
bonds, and of any orders or responses relating thereto by the registrar or
court, and establish and maintain a system for indexing, filing, or recording
which is sufficient to enable users of the records to obtain adequate
information. Upon payment of the fees required by law the clerk must issue
certified copies of any probated wills, letters issued to personal
representatives, or any other record or paper filed or recorded. Certificates
relating to probated wills must indicate whether the decedent was domiciled in
this state and whether the probate was formal or informal. Certificates
relating to letters must show the date of appointment.
