59-1-1005.   Suits against commission and its employees.
(1)  A taxpayer may bring a civil suit against the commission for recovery of
actual damages and costs incurred by the taxpayer if:
(a)  the commission or one of its employees intentionally or recklessly takes
possession of a taxpayer's property in disregard of its published procedures,
laws, or rules; or
(b)  otherwise intentionally or recklessly disregards published procedures,
laws, or rules.
(2)  An award of actual damages and court costs in a suit under this section
may not exceed $100,000.
(3)  If the court finds that the civil action brought by the taxpayer is
frivolous, the court may impose a penalty of up to $10,000 against the
taxpayer.
