10-3-918.   Chief of police or marshal in a city of the third, fourth, or
fifth class or town.
The chief of police or marshal in each city of the third, fourth, or fifth
class or town:
(1)  shall:
(a)  exercise and perform the duties that are prescribed by the legislative
body;
(b)  be under the direction, control, and supervision of the person or body
that appointed the chief or marshal; and
(c)  on or before January 1, 2003, adopt a written policy that prohibits the
stopping, detention, or search of any person when the action is solely
motivated by considerations of race, color, ethnicity, age, or gender; and
(2)  may, with the consent of the person or body that appointed the chief or
marshal, appoint assistants to the chief of police or marshal.
